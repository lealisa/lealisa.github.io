Creating a digital museum for the MoonArk time capsule

  • Team

    Zac Mau

  • Responsibilities

    UX/UI, Prototyping, Visual Design & Front-end Development

In 2021, Carnegie Mellon University is sending the first museum to the Moon aboard an Astrobotic lander. The museum, called the MoonArk, is an architectural sculpture designed by a team of CMU faculty, students and a host of international collaborators over the past 10 years. Weighing only 8 ounces, it contains hundreds of images, poems, musical compositions, nano-objects, mechanisms, and earthly samples, all intertwined to tell the story of humanity’s relationship with the moon.

My partner and I worked closely with the creators of the MoonArk to design and build an interactive website to serve as a virtual companion to the artifact. The goal of the website was to not only document all the pieces, but to also help visitors learn about the complex narrative of the MoonArk and to inspire people to engage artistically with the moon.
