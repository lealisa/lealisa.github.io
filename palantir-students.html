Designing and building student recruitment website

  • What I did

    Web and Visual design, Art direction, Front-end development

  • July–August 2018

Every year, Palantir creates a branded campaign for the college recruitment season. In summer 2018, I got to collaborate with Palantir’s communication team to design and implement the student recruitment website to serve as a digital component to the physical swag and materials.

This side project was a fun addition to my product work as it provided an opportunity to not only flex my web design and front-end development skills, but allowed me to do some illustration and art direction.

Context & process

While Palantir’s communication team designed a new fun campaign for the recruitment season every year, there wasn’t always a digital counterpart. The standard student openings web page was not very engaging and outdated, and didn’t match the theme of the other assets. I was approached by the communication design team to create a new version of the page that was not only engaging visually, but provided more context about Palantir’s products and work to prospective applicants.

Screenshot of Palantir’s satudent page prior to the redesign.

The task was pretty open ended, so I got to decide on the content I wanted to display on the page. I worked closely with copy writters to establish the right tone and message. Visually, as a starting point, I was given assets for the physical banners and color swatches of that year’s brand that I worked to incorporate into the web design.

Palantir’s 2018 stiudent recruitment branding designed by the communication design team.

Final design

For the final design of the page, I ended up inverting the colors from the campaign for readability, as well as designing more illustrations to match the ones from the physical branding. I also art directed the animations used to illustrate different industries that Palantir works in.

Bonus: Animated illustrations

By housing actions within categories, there is ample room for descriptions and contextual illustrations, helping users learn what they are able to do.

While the branding of the webpage has changed since to reflect most up to date campaign, most of the buolding blocks I designed and implemented have been reused. The link to this year's recruitment website is below.
