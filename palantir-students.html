Designing and building Palantir's student recruitment website

  • Responsibilities

    Web and Visual design, Art direction, Front-end development

  • July–August 2018

Every year, Palantir creates a branded campaign for the college recruitment season. In the summer of 2018, I partnered with Palantir’s communication team to design and implement the student recruitment website. The website is used in tandem with physical and print materials to help promote Palantir’s culture during university events.

This side project was a fun addition to my product work as it provided an opportunity to not only flex my web design and front-end development skills, but allowed me to do some illustration and art direction.

Context & process

Palantir’s communication team is responsible for designing a new campaign for the annual recruitment season, but there are not always enough resources to build a new site every year. In 2018, the available Student Openings web page was outdated and didn’t match the theme of the other assets. I was approached to create a new version of the page that was not only visually exciting, but provided more context about Palantir’s products and work to prospective applicants.

In addition to what the communication team was looking for, I had a few goals in mind for this project. I wanted to bring more educational content into the page so students would have an easier time understand what Palantir does, and I wanted to engineer the site in a way that it would be reusable in the future.

Screenshot of Palantir’s student page prior to the redesign.

Since this was a relatively bluesky project, I was able to pick and choose what content I wanted to display on the page. I worked closely with content strategists and internal recruiters to craft a message and tone of voice that I felt matched Palantir’s culture.

There were also a few physical assets that I was given from the communications team, including a banner and color swatches, which I used as inspiration for the visual direction.

Palantir’s 2018 student recruitment branding designed by the communication design team.

Final design

For the final design, I decided to leverage the list of industries provided to me by the communications team and turn it into an illustrative value proposition. The print materials used for recruiting are often unable to comprehensively explain the specifics of what Palantir does. This interactive webpage was a great opportunity to demonstrate what Palantir does through specific usecases.

With the visual design, I chose to invert the colors from the campaign for readability. I also created more illustrations for the website to match the ones from the physical branding.

Bonus: Animated illustrations

This project also gave me the unique opportunity to work clsoely with one of our motion designers to art direct these contextual animations.
