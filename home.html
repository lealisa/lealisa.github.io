About

During my time at Palantir Technologies1 I designed enterprise software for commercial, philanthropic, and government institutions around the globe. I drove design on tools like Contour, a top-down data analysis application, and Gaia, a geospacial analysis platform used for mission planning and disaster relief. I worked closely with various departments of the company, including Engineering, Business Development, and Marketing, as well as product owners, stakeholders, and users.

Outside of my core responsibilities, I got to work on a cross-team effort to improve the ecosystem experience for the commercial platform, and travel on-site to collaborate with clients to iterate, design and implement custom dashboards for product demos. I was also involved with recruiting and team building, acted as a mentor for design interns and new hires, and taught design methods to Palantir engineers as part of the Product Design University bootcamp.

Prior to Palantir, I was designing voice-driven apps for the visually impaired with Conversant Labs2, and building a digital museum for the MoonArk3. I earned my degree in Communication Design from Carnegie Mellon University4, where I discovered my love for creating interactive experiences, as well as front-end development, visual design, and motion. In my work, I enjoy being involved in every step of the design process—from strategy and ideation, all the way through prototyping and aligning the tiniest pixel in code.

Born in Moscow to Russian-Vietnamese parents, and immigrating to the U.S. in high school, I’ve always been interested in how people bridge gaps between different languages, cultures and backgrounds. In college, I became fascinated with how design can not only drive positive change, but can also bring people of diverse identities and experiences together.
