Designing an educational game
for PNC bank to teach clients
about long-term investment

  • Team

    Leah Anton & Sharon Yu

  • Role

    UX/UI, Visual design

  • January–April 2017

Gain is a board game with an accompanying mobile app designed to playfully simulate the experience of investing money. Through playing the game, people can learn about the benefits of long-term financial management. The goal of Gain is to help PNC’s clients feel more confident sharing their financial goals with the institution in order to enhance investment opportunities.

This project was sponsored by PNC in partnership with the Carnegie Mellon School of Design as part of the Learning Experience Design course.

Research & process

Investing is one of the best ways to build long-term wealth, but a lot of people miss out on this experience because they believe that it's only for affluent people and don’t realize the breadth of options available to them.

We were approached by PNC to design an experience that would encourage customers to share personal information with the institution in order to receive personalized banking advice. We began our process using personas and preliminary research that the client had done in order to better define the problem space. From there we set out to synthesize and validate their findings, and conduct our own research to fill in the gaps.

Examples of some of the exercises we did in order to identify design opportunities based on client’s initial research and to understand types of investments available to different customers.

Through contextual research and dissecting the information we received, we quickly found that there are several learning gaps between the customers and our client. We reframed our observations into “how might we” statements to allow ourselves turn these problems into design opportunities.

Challenges
  • Customers feel like they don’t receive tailored experiences from the bank, giving them little reason to trust it with investment options.
  • There is a lack of understanding of the services that the bank has to offer and the positive impact it could have on a customer's financial wellbeing.
  • Customers vary greatly in age, investing experience, and financial literacy.
Goal

How might we empower customers to confidently invest money with PNC bank according to their financial goals, circumstances, and needs?

With this goal in mind, we decided to focus on educating customers about their unique investment options and personalized long-term planning. Our hypothesis was that with more financial literacy, customers will be at ease to have meaningful exchanges with the institution.

Through researching experiential learning methods, gamification theories, and the psychology behind money management and investing, we found that the best way for people to engage with such serious material would be to simulate it in a low-stakes, low-pressure environment. A large number of surveyed customers expressed preference for in-person consultations and interactions, so we wanted to make sure that the experience had both physical and digital components.

Brainstorming game play and physical board format

After exploring a range of ideas through creating storyboards, user journeys, and conducting workshops with client’s design team and financial advisors, we set off to create an educational board game. Our team felt that a board game would be a wholesome, age-neutral medium that would allow customers to experience investing in a fun and engaging way.

Final game

Our final design proposal for PNC consisted of the Gain board game and companion app. By combining a short-term interactive experience with a long-term digital presence we could incentivize customers to engage with PNC across multiple touchpoints. The board game would act as an introduction to investment strategies, and the app would provide the customer with opportunities to use what they learned during gameplay.

Getting started

To begin, the Gain app introduces each player to the rules of the game, and gives them a quick rundown on what the Dreams, Needs, and Chance cards are. Players then pick four Dream cards each from a deck and place them on their Dashboard; these “dreams” act as each player’s personal set of challenges they need to overcome to win the game.

The main goal of the game is to achieve all 4 of the Dreams—which includes real world examples like buying a house or paying for children’s education—before the other players do.

Learning to invest

In order to stress the importance of diversifying assets, the only way to earn enough money to accomplish the Dreams is by investing money through various means including stocks, bonds, IRAs, and etc.

Challenges

While users are moving their pieces around the board they will also encounter Chance cards and Needs cards, which can provide surprise income from events like inheritance or winning a local lottery, or induce unexepected costs like parking tickets and surgeries. These cards help emphasize that players need to do more than save money, they need to invest to help them cover unforseen costs.

Mobile app

The Gain mobile app was designed as a companion to the board game for those players that are more digitally competent.

During gameplay, the app acts as a guidebook to teach players the rules of the game and define any terms that players may be unfamiliar with. The app also tracks a player’s in-game net worth and investing portfolio and dynamically adjusts the value of their stocks to mimic the way the market fluctuates in real life.

The app guides players through a character creation, allowing them to select things like their marital status, children, etc. Selection affects the gameplay in order to teach players about the consequences.

The resources tab allows players to quickly look up definitions for terms they might not be familiar with, without breaking the flow of the game.

The Finances tab shows players’ progress through the game and the breakdown of their assets, as well as makes helpful suggestions about investing.

After the game has ended, players can use the app to stay in touch with bank’s dedicated financial advisors, so they can use what they learned in the game to begin investing through PNC.
