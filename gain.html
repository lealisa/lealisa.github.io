Gain is a board game with an accompanying mobile app designed to playfully simulate the experience of investing money. Through playing the game, people can learn about the benefits of long-term financial management. The goal of the game is to help PNC’s clients feel more confident sharing their financial goals with the institution in order to enhance investment opportunities.

This project was sponsored by PNC in partnership with the Carnegie Mellon School of Design as part of the Learning Experience Design course.

Research & process

Investing is one of the best ways to build long-term wealth, but a lot of people miss out on this experience because they believe that it's only for affluent people and don’t realize the breadth of options available to them.

We were approached by PNC to design an experience that would encourage customers to share personal information with the institution in order to receive personalized banking advice. We began our process using personas and preliminary research that the client had done in order to better define the problem space. From there we set out to synthesize and validate their findings, and conduct our own research to fill in the gaps.

Identifying design opportunities based on client’s initial research

Breaking down personas and identifying types of investments available to different customers

Through contextual research and dissecting the information we received, we quickly found that there are several learning gaps between the customers and our client. We reframed our observations into “how might we” statements to allow ourselves turn these problems into design opportunities.

Challenges
  • Customers feel like they don’t receive tailored experiences from the bank, giving them little reason to trust it with investment options
  • There is a lack of understanding of the services that the bank has to offer and the positive impact it could have on financial wellbeing
  • Customers vary greatly in age, investing experience, and financial literacy
Goal

How might we empower customers to confidently invest money with PNC bank according to their financial goals, circumstances and needs?

With this goal in mind, we decided to focus on educating customers about their investment options and long-term monetary planning to stress pursuit of their financial wellbeing. Our hypothesis was that with more financial literacy, customers will be at ease to have meaningful exchanges with the institution.

Through researching experiential learning methods, gamification theories, and the psychology behind money management and investing, we found that the best way for people to engage with such serious material would be to simulate it in a low-stakes, low-pressure environment. A large number of surveyed customers expressed preference for in-person consultations and interactions, so we wanted to make sure that the experience had both physical and digital components.

Brainstorming game play and physical board format

After exploring a range of ideas through creating storyboards, user journeys and conducting workshops with client’s design team and financial advisors, we set off to create an educational board game. Our team felt that a board game would be a wholesome, age-neutral medium that would allow customers to experience investing in a fun and engaging way.

Final design

For the final product we landed on a playful tone of voice for the design, which provided a welcome contrast to the otherwise serious topic. This aesthetic is is constant across the both mobile app, which introduces users to the rules of the game and provides subsequent investing tips, and the board game itself. This visual unity ties the two distinct aspects of Gain together in a cohesive and fun way across multiple points of engagement.
