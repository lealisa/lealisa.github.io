    About

    1 Palantir is a software company that builds data fusion platforms for integrating, managing, and securing data, at massive scale.

    3 Conversant Labs was a Pittsburgh-based startup that focused on developing audio-only applications tailored to the specific needs of the visually impaired and blind.

    2 MoonArk is a time capsule created by a multidisciplinary team of artists and engineers, set to arrive on the Moon in 2021.

    4 Carnegie Mellon University is a private research university based in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.

    I am currently working at Palantir Technologies1, designing software and tools that empower some of the world’s crucial organizations to ask and answer their most complex questions.

    Prior to Palantir, I was designing voice-driven apps for the visually impaired with Conversant Labs2, and building a digital museum for the MoonArk3. I earned my degree in Communication Design from Carnegie Mellon University4, where I discovered my love for creating interactive experiences, as well as front-end development, print, motion and furniture design.

    Born in Moscow to Russian-Vietnamese parents, and spending most of my formative years in the U.S., I’ve always been interested in how people bridge gaps between different languages, cultures and backgrounds. In college, I became fascinated with how design can not only drive positive change, but can also bring people of diverse identities and experiences together.
